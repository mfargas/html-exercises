Matt Groening

Matt Groening speaking at a ComicCon Panel.

The legendary Matt Groening is an American cartoonist who created the Simpsons among many other adult cartoons. Groening revolutionized the adult cartoon genre and created iconic characters that have legacies like no other.

You may recognize his creations ranging from Netflix original Disenchantment to his most famous line of work The Simpsons . My personal favorite would be Futurama however I believe it is drastically underrated and in The Simpsons's shadow.


Here is a timeline of Matt Groening's Accomplishments:

  • 1954: Matthew Abraham Groening was born February 15th to Margaret Ruth, a teacher, and Homer Groening, a writer, filmmaker, and advertiser in Portland, Oregon.
  • 1977: Graduated from Evergreen State College after having spent 5 years studying there. From there, he moved to Los Angeles.
  • 1980: PublishedLife In Hell, a comic strip he created two years prior. Life In Hell sparked a full series of comic strips that were an instant success.
  • 1987: Worked with the Tracey Ullman Show as a writer and animator, which resulted in the creation of the Simpsons. At the time, the Simpsons ran as an animated series to air on the Tracey Ullman Show.
  • 1989: The Simpsons was officially born when the Tracey Ullman Show was cancelled two years prior and the show earned a spin-off series. Groening worked as the creator, writer, voice actor, and executive producer of the Simpsons.
  • 1994: Started the comic book publishing company named 'Bongo Comics'. This led to the founding of 'Zongo Comics' the following year.
  • 1999: Futurama aired after its creation in 1997 and years of sci-fi research. The show initially aired under the Fox network. Groening worked as creator, writer, executive producer, and voice actor for the show, spending time away from the Simpsons for the time.
  • 2003: Futurama was cancelled by Fox, yet maintained its massive following.
  • 2007: Served as executive producer for the four Futurama films released in 2007, 2008, and 2009.
  • 2010: Futurama was reborn for an additional 3 years.
  • 2012: Served as writer and executive producer of a 3D short film, 'The Longest Daycare' based on the Simpsons
  • 2018: Created Disenchantment, his first project with a serialized story format serving a chronological narrative. This is Groening's first project to air on a streaming service, Netflix.
